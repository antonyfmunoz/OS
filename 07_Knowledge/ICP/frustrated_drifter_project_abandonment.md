# ICP Insight Entry

## Signal Source

Raw signal (unattributed)

---

## Signal Summary

Person with a habitual pattern of starting projects and never finishing them. Has connected unfinished work to a sense of wasted life — the pain is identity-level, not just productivity-level.

---

## Pain Pattern

Chronic project abandonment driven by shiny object syndrome, lack of completion structure, or fear of imperfect output. The loop: start with excitement → momentum fades → abandon → feel shame → repeat. Shame compounds over time into identity damage ("I'm someone who doesn't finish things").

---

## Desired Transformation

To finish things. To look back and see completed work, not a graveyard of half-built ideas. Underneath that: to feel like their life is being spent, not wasted.

---

## Language Patterns

- "I keep starting" — habitual, not a one-time failure; they've already noticed the pattern
- "never finishing them" — absolute framing, high internal judgment
- "makes me feel like I'm wasting my life" — stakes are identity-level, not productivity-level
- "wasting my life" — not annoyed, scared; this is the emotional hook

---

## Psychological State

Frustrated Drifter

Moves between projects on enthusiasm rather than structure. Not lazy — likely high-energy and curious. Without a system to carry projects through the dip, they abandon before completion. Frustration is self-directed and building toward an identity crisis.

---

## ICP Match

High

Actively in pain about unfinished output. Not browsing for tips — needs a system that holds them to completion. High urgency, high emotional readiness to act.

---

## Content Opportunities

- "Starting is easy. Finishing is a skill. Here's how to build it."
- "The reason you don't finish projects isn't discipline — it's that you have no bridge from excitement to execution."
- "Every unfinished project is a loan against your future self-belief."
- Myth-bust: "Starting more projects won't fix a finishing problem."
- Discussion prompt: "How many half-finished projects are sitting in your notes right now?"
- Vulnerable angle: "I used to abandon everything I started — here's what actually changed that."

---

## Outreach Opportunities

- Open with: "Do you find it easier to start things than to finish them?" — low-stakes, highly relatable
- Reframe: The problem isn't motivation or discipline — it's the absence of a completion system. Once excitement fades, there's nothing structural to carry them through.
- Follow-up angle: Ask what their most recent abandoned project was and what happened when the momentum died — that answer reveals the exact intervention point.
