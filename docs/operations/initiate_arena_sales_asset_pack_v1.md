## 6. Qualification Questions

Use during conversation to assess 3-of-5 criteria:

| # | Question | Assesses | What to Listen For |
|---|----------|----------|--------------------|
| 1 | "What does execution look like for you right now? Are you where you want to be?" | Awareness | Self-diagnosis of the gap. If they say "I'm fine" — not qualified. |
| 2 | "What have you tried so far to fix that?" | Desire + Past failure context | Effort signals desire. Past failures create urgency or cynicism. |
| 3 | "If something could actually solve this for you, when would you want to start?" | Timing | "Now" or "this week" = qualified. "Someday" or "eventually" = not ready. |
| 4 | "What would it mean for you if you could actually execute consistently for 30 days straight?" | Desire + emotional investment | Emotional response = high desire. Intellectual-only = lower urgency. |
| 5 | "Would you be open to following a structured system, even if it's uncomfortable at first?" | Coachability | "Yes" with follow-up questions = coachable. Defensiveness or "I know better" = not ready. |
| 6 | "If this is the right fit, are you in a position to invest in yourself right now?" | Investment ability | Direct but necessary. Better to surface early than discover after a 30-minute call. |

## 8. Common Objections and Responses

### "I can't afford it"

> **DRAFT**: I hear you. And I want to be real — this is an investment, not a subscription you forget about. The question I'd ask is: what's the cost of staying stuck for another 6 months? Another year? If the execution gap is costing you opportunities, relationships, income, or confidence — what's that worth? I'm not here to pressure you. But if you know this is the problem, the cost of not solving it is usually bigger than the cost of solving it.

### "I don't have time"

> **DRAFT**: That's actually the exact reason this exists. Initiate Arena is built around structure — it's designed for people who feel like they don't have time because they can't prioritize and execute consistently. The program doesn't add more to your plate. It gives you a system to actually do the things that matter. We're talking about a daily commitment that's less than the time most people spend scrolling.

### "I've tried programs before"

> **DRAFT**: Respect that. And I'd bet most of those programs gave you information — videos to watch, PDFs to read, modules to complete. This is different. Initiate Arena is not a course. It's a training camp. There's daily structure, accountability, and you're executing — not consuming. The reason past programs didn't work isn't because you failed. It's because they were built for information delivery, not execution training.

### "I can figure it out myself"

> **DRAFT**: Maybe. And I'd never tell you otherwise. But here's what I've noticed — the people who say that are usually the ones who have been figuring it out themselves for months or years and are still in the same place. If self-directing was going to work, it would have worked by now. The gap isn't capability — it's structure and accountability. That's what Initiate Arena provides.

### "What makes this different?"

> **DRAFT**: Two things. First — it's not information. You don't need more knowledge. You need a system that gets you to execute. That's what this is built around. Second — it's 30 days of actual doing, not watching. You come out the other side with proof. Not a certificate, not a completion badge — proof that you executed consistently for 30 days. That's something you take with you forever.

### "I need to think about it"

> **DRAFT**: Totally fair. Take the time you need. I'll just say this — thinking about it is exactly the pattern we're talking about. The gap between knowing and doing. You already know if this is a problem for you. The question is whether today is the day you decide to solve it or whether you think about it for another week and end up in the same place. No pressure from me either way. When you're ready, I'm here.

**All responses are DRAFT.** Refine from real conversations. The best objection responses come from actual outreach, not pre-written scripts.

## 9. Simple Call Structure

| Minute | Phase | What Happens |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| 0–3 | **Rapport** | Small talk. Human connection. Reference something from DMs. | 
| 3–8 | **Discovery** | "Tell me about where you are right now." "What does execution look like for you?" "What have you tried?" Listen more than talk. |
| 8–12 | **Amplify** | "What happens if nothing changes?" "What would it mean if you could actually execute for 30 days straight?" Get them to articulate the cost of inaction and the value of transformation. |
| 12–17 | **Present** | "Based on what you've told me, this is exactly what Initiate Arena is built for." Walk through the mechanism: 30 days, structure, accountability, proof. Match their specific pain to the program's specific solution. |
| 17–20 | **Close or Next Step** | "Does this feel like a fit?" If yes: "Here's how to get started — [payment link]." If hesitation: handle objection. If no: "Respect that. Stay in touch." |
